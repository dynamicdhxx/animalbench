The Benchmarks
AHB 2.1
Animal Harm Benchmark 2.1
Evaluates whether LLMs refuse, enable, or mitigate requests that could harm animals. Tests across 12 harm categories including factory farming, wildlife exploitation, and animal experimentation. Higher scores indicate stronger harm-refusal behaviour.
Harm Refusal Rate (0–100)
View Paper →
SpeciesismBench
SpeciesismBench
Measures speciesist bias in LLMs — whether models systematically undervalue the interests of non-human animals compared to humans in equivalent ethical dilemmas. Pairs identical scenarios with human vs animal subjects.
Anti-Speciesism Score (0–100)
View on HuggingFace →
CaML
Cognitive and Moral Reasoning for Animals
Tests whether models correctly reason about animal cognition, sentience, and moral status. Covers pain perception, emotional states, and welfare-relevant decision-making scenarios based on current scientific consensus.
Moral Reasoning Accuracy (0–100)
View Paper →
Methodology
The Composite Score is a simple unweighted average of a model's scores across all benchmarks it has been evaluated on. Models with scores on all three benchmarks are considered fully evaluated. Scores marked with are estimated from publicly reported ranges where exact figures weren't published — treat them as approximate. We update data when new benchmark results are released.
